Bio

Ayrton Senna da Silva (March 21, 1960 – May 1, 1994), better known as Ayrton Senna, was a Brazilian Formula One driver who won the world championship three times, in 1988, 1990 and in 1991. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest and most skilful drivers of all time. He died in 1994, at the peak of his motor racing career in a tragic accident at Imola, San Marino.



Career History

  • 1984 - Debuted in Formula 1 with Toleman. Got 2nd place in his first ever race in Monaco during rain, beating other experienced driver such as Niki Lauda and Nelson Piquet.
  • 1986 - Clenched his first ever win at GP Estoril in Portugal with Lotus F1 Team.
  • 1988 - Moved to McLaren and become world champion in 1988 after defeated Alain Prost.
  • 1989 - Became 2 times world champion with McLaren.
  • 1991 - Became 3 times world champion with McLaren.
  • 1993 - Last race with McLaren and also last win for Ayrton Senna.
  • 1994 - Moved to Williams with Damon Hill as teammate.
  • 1994 - Died because of crash in Tamborello chicane, Imola Circuit, San Marino.
